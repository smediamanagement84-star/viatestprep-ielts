PTE Speaking Mastery
6–7 items 30–40 sec each Scores: Speaking + Reading Highest value task

A text of up to 60 words appears on screen. After a countdown and beep you read it aloud. This is the single most impactful task in PTE — it scores Speaking (fluency, pronunciation) AND Reading (accuracy) simultaneously.

What The AI Scores
SUB-SKILL WHAT IT MEASURES
Content Percentage of words read correctly and in order
Oral Fluency Natural rhythm, smooth chunked phrasing — no hesitations or false starts
Pronunciation Correct sound production, word stress, and connected speech
How To Attempt — Step By Step
1
Use all prep time wisely
Skim the full text once. Identify unfamiliar words and rehearse them silently. Mark natural pause points at commas and full stops.
2
Begin immediately after the beep
Start within 1 second. Dead air at the beginning registers as a fluency issue.
3
Read in meaning chunks — not word by word
Group words into phrases: "The government / has announced / new investment policies / for rural development." Pausing at punctuation creates natural rhythm.
4
Stress content words, reduce function words
Emphasise nouns, main verbs, adjectives. Reduce articles (a, the), prepositions (of, in). This is how native English rhythm works.
5
Never stop or self-correct
If you mispronounce a word, keep going. Self-correction destroys fluency more than the original error.
Chunking Guide
READ IN MEANING CHUNKS: ✗ Wrong: "The / risk / of / flooding / in / coastal / areas / is / increasing."
✓ Correct: The risk of flooding / in coastal areas / is increasing / at an alarming rate. Pause rule: Comma = brief pause (0.3s) · Full stop = longer pause (0.5s)
Stress rule: Give extra volume + length to the main noun in each chunk:
"The RISK of flooding / in COASTal areas / is inCREASing."
Pronunciation Rules For 79+
KEY RULES
▸
Linking: Connect words — "turn_it_on", "an_apple", "at_all". Choppy delivery drops fluency.
▸
Schwa: Unstressed vowels reduce to /ə/ — "about" = /əˈbaʊt/.
▸
Syllable stress: pho-TOG-ra-phy · e-CON-o-my · gov-ERN-ment.
What To Avoid
COMMON MISTAKES
✗
Going silent mid-text — the AI cuts the microphone after 3 seconds of silence.
✗
Repeating or correcting yourself — self-correction destroys fluency.
✗
Skipping words or lines — each skipped word reduces content score.

10–12 items 15 sec to respond Scores: Speaking + Listening 2nd highest value task

An audio sentence plays once. You must repeat it exactly — same words, same order, same rhythm. With 10–12 items it is the most frequently occurring task in the test, and simultaneously scores Speaking and Listening.

What The AI Scores
SUB-SKILL WHAT IT MEASURES
Content Percentage of original words reproduced correctly and in the right order
Oral Fluency Smooth, uninterrupted delivery at natural pace — no choppy hesitation
Pronunciation Correct sounds and stress patterns matching the original speaker
How To Attempt — Step By Step
1
Listen without writing or typing
Full attention on audio only. Writing shorthand means you will miss the second half of the sentence.
2
Use the 4-Block Memory Method
Anchor the first 2-3 words → capture subject + verb core → hold the last 3-4 words → reconstruct.
3
Speak immediately after audio ends
Begin within 1 second. The microphone activates automatically. Pausing longer than 2 seconds costs fluency.
Memory Template
Example: "The government has allocated significant funding for renewable energy development." BLOCK 1 (Anchor): The government has
BLOCK 2 (Core): allocated significant funding
BLOCK 3 (Tail): for renewable energy development
3–4 items 25 sec prep · 40 sec speak Scores: Speaking

An image appears — bar chart, line graph, pie chart, table, map, or process diagram. You have 25 seconds to study it then 40 seconds to describe it. You must fill the full 40 seconds without stopping.

Universal 5-Sentence Template
① INTRODUCTION (5–6 sec): "The [image type] illustrates [main topic]." ② OVERVIEW — Key Trend (7–8 sec): "Overall, it is evident that [most striking finding or dominant pattern]." ③ HIGHEST / MOST PROMINENT (8 sec): "Notably, [category with highest value] recorded the highest figure at approximately [value]." ④ LOWEST / CONTRAST (7–8 sec): "In contrast, [lowest category] showed the smallest figure at [value]." ⑤ CONCLUSION (6–7 sec): "In conclusion, the [image type] clearly demonstrates that [key takeaway]."
2–3 items Audio up to 90 sec Scores: Speaking + Listening

An audio lecture plays. After it ends you have 10 seconds to prepare then 40 seconds to summarise it in your own words. Focus on the main topic, central argument, and 2-3 supporting points.

5-Sentence Response Template
① TOPIC (5 sec): "The lecture discusses [main topic]." ② MAIN ARGUMENT (8 sec): "The speaker argues that [central claim]." ③ SUPPORTING DETAIL 1 (8 sec): "Furthermore, [first key supporting point]." ④ SUPPORTING DETAIL 2 (8 sec): "The speaker also mentions that [second key point]." ⑤ CONCLUSION (7 sec): "In conclusion, the lecture highlights the importance of [key takeaway]."
5–6 items 3–9 sec audio Scores: Listening + Speaking

A short question plays. You answer with one word or a very short phrase. One correct word = full marks.

Examples
QUESTION TYPE EXAMPLE → ANSWER
Opposites "What is the opposite of expensive?" → cheap / inexpensive
Definitions "What do you call a doctor who treats children?" → paediatrician
Science "What gas do plants absorb?" → carbon dioxide / CO₂
2–3 items (Academic) Scores: Speaking

You hear and read a real-world situation. You have 10 seconds to prepare, then 40 seconds to speak. Respond naturally as you would to a real person.

4-Part Template
OPENER: "[Hi / Hello / Good morning], I [wanted to reach out] about [the situation]." MAIN RESPONSE: "I [apologise for / wanted to explain] [specific detail]." SOLUTION / NEXT STEP: "I think the best way to resolve this would be to [practical step]." POLITE CLOSE: "Please [let me know] if you need anything further."
2–3 items Scores: Listening + Speaking

You listen to a 3-speaker conversation (up to 3 minutes) then have 2 minutes to summarise the main viewpoints.

5-Sentence Response Template
① INTRODUCE TOPIC: "The group discussed [main topic], exploring a range of perspectives." ② FIRST VIEWPOINT: "One speaker argued that [Viewpoint A]." ③ SECOND VIEWPOINT: "Another participant pointed out that [Viewpoint B]." ④ THIRD VIEWPOINT: "A third perspective raised was that [Viewpoint C]." ⑤ OVERALL CONCLUSION: "Overall, the discussion highlighted [central tension]."
