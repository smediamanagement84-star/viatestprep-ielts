② What is IELTS?

IELTS — the International English Language Testing System — is one of the most widely accepted English-proficiency tests in the world. It is jointly run by three organisations and accepted by over 11,000 institutions worldwide.

Who takes IELTS and why?

🎓 Studying abroad
Most universities in the UK, Australia, Canada, NZ and many in the US require an IELTS Academic score.
🌍 Immigration
Canada, Australia, NZ, UK use IELTS for skilled-worker visas and permanent residency applications.
💼 Work / Professional
Doctors, nurses, engineers and teachers often need IELTS to register with professional bodies abroad.

③ Academic or General Training?

Two versions of IELTS exist. The Listening and Speaking sections are identical. Reading and Writing differ.

Academic

IELTS Academic

Take this if you want to:

  • Study at a university (any level)
  • Register as a professional (doctor, nurse, engineer)
  • Join graduate or postgraduate programs

What's different:

  • Reading: 3 academic passages (journals, articles, textbooks)
  • Writing Task 1: Describe a chart, graph, map or process
  • Writing Task 2: Academic essay
General Training

IELTS General Training

Take this if you want to:

  • Migrate to Australia, Canada, NZ or UK
  • Work in an English-speaking country (non-professional)
  • Do secondary education or training abroad

What's different:

  • Reading: Notices, ads, workplace docs (easier register)
  • Writing Task 1: Write a letter (formal / semi-formal / informal)
  • Writing Task 2: Essay (slightly easier topics)
💡 Quick rule of thumb: University = Academic. Visa / migration = General Training. If you're unsure, always check the official requirement of the institution/country you're applying to.

④ The Test Format — 2h 45min

All four sections happen on the same day. Listening, Reading, and Writing run back-to-back without a break. Speaking is often the same day (afternoon) or up to 7 days before/after.

🎧 Listening30 min + 10 transfer
📖 Reading60 min
✍ Writing60 min (T1: 20 + T2: 40)
🎙 Speaking11–14 min
Reading + Writing happen back-to-back with no break. Bring focus stamina.

⑤ How Scoring Works — The 0–9 Band Scale

IELTS doesn't give pass/fail. You get a band score from 0 to 9 for each of the four skills, plus an overall band (the average, rounded to the nearest 0.5). Each whole band represents a clear level of English ability.

How the Overall Band is Calculated

Your four scores are averaged. The result is rounded to the nearest 0.5.

Example: L 7.5 · R 7.0 · W 6.0 · S 7.0 = average 6.875 → rounded to Band 7.0.

.25 rounds up to the next half; .75 rounds up to the next whole. So 6.125 = 6.0, but 6.25 = 6.5.

What Band Do You Need?

🎓 University (undergraduate): usually 6.0–6.5 overall, with no skill below 5.5 or 6.0.
🎓 University (postgraduate): usually 6.5–7.0 overall, often with 6.5 in Writing.
🌍 Skilled migration (Canada/Australia): usually 6.0–7.0 per skill.
💼 Medical / nursing registration: often 7.0 or 7.5 in each skill — the highest bar.
💡 Always check your exact target. Different programs and visa categories have different rules. The Band 7+ Track in this course aims to get you safely past the highest commonly required threshold.

⑦ Test Day — What to Expect

Before you go

What to bring

  • Original passport (or national ID, depending on country) — same one used at booking
  • Confirmation email/letter (some centres)
  • Pencils + an eraser (provided in many centres, bring your own as backup)
  • A clear water bottle (no label)

What NOT to bring: phones, smartwatches, books, notes, calculator, food, coloured pens. All these stay in lockers.

Timeline

A typical morning

  1. ~30 min before: arrive, register, photo + fingerprint, locker for belongings.
  2. Listening (30 min + 10 transfer): headphones, played once.
  3. Reading (60 min): three passages, 40 questions, no extra transfer time.
  4. Writing (60 min): Task 1 (20 min), Task 2 (40 min).
  5. Speaking (11–14 min): face-to-face with an examiner, often same-day afternoon or another day within a week.

📅 Results & Re-takes

Paper-based: results in 13 days.
Computer-based: results in 3–5 days.
Re-take: no waiting period. You can also retake just ONE skill (IELTS One Skill Retake) within 60 days, in many countries.
