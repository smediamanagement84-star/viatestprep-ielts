Lesson 03 · Writing

You are not an artist. You are an architect.

The IELTS Writing test is not a creative competition. Your essay is a structure — logical, strong, fit for purpose. Beautiful English fails when the blueprint is wrong. Today you stop "writing" and start building. Specs first. Bricks later.

⏱ 60 min · 2 tasks 🎯 Band 7+ target 📐 Structure-first

T2 250+ words · 40 min · Argumentative essay · Worth 2× Task 1

② The Four Pillars (= Your Score)

Each = 25%

Before you write a single word, know the four ways you're being judged. To hit 7+ you need balance across all four — not excellence in one.

Pillar 1

Task Achievement / Task Response

Did you answer the question? The #1 reason candidates lose marks. Address every part of the prompt. Maintain a clear position throughout.

Watch for Multi-part prompts. "Discuss BOTH views AND give your opinion" = 3 things.
Pillar 2

Coherence & Cohesion

Is it logical and easy to follow? The skeleton. Clear paragraphs, one central idea each, smooth linking.

Watch for Mechanical linkers ("Firstly… Secondly… Thirdly…"). Examiners notice.
Pillar 3

Lexical Resource

Right words, not big words. Precision, collocations, paraphrasing. Don't reach for words you can't control.

Watch for Repeating the prompt's keywords. Paraphrase or pay the price.
Pillar 4

Grammatical Range & Accuracy

Range is what builds the skyscraper. Mix simple and complex sentences, conditionals, passives, relative clauses. Accurately.

Watch for Showing off complex grammar incorrectly costs more than playing safe.
Pillar drill

Diagnose the Weakness

Read each sentence. Click the pillar that's weakest. Train your editor's eye.

③ Task 1 — The Data Report (20 min · 150+ words)

Report. Don't interpret.

Your only job: be a human camera. Describe what you see. No opinions, no reasons, no interpretations.

P1
Introduction

1 sentence. Paraphrase the title.

P2 · CRITICAL
Overview

2 sentences. Big-picture trends. NO numbers.

P3
Detail #1

3–4 sentences. Group A data with numbers.

P4
Detail #2

3–4 sentences. Group B data with comparisons.

Pillar 2 secret: The OVERVIEW is what separates Band 6 from Band 7. Most candidates skip it or load it with numbers. Don't.

④ Task 2 — The Argumentative Essay (40 min · 250+ words)

Worth 2/3 of your Writing score

The most important 40 minutes of your test. Always do Task 2 first. The first 10 minutes are for the plan — not the prose.

⏱ The Golden 10-Minute Plan

Minutes 0–2
Deconstruct the prompt

Identify topic, task type (opinion / discussion / advantages / problem-solution / two-part), keywords.

Minutes 2–10
Build the blueprint

4-paragraph outline. Each body para: 2–3 ideas with examples. Then write — fast.

The Bulletproof 4-Paragraph Build

P1
Introduction (~50 w)

Paraphrase prompt → state thesis.

P2
Body 1 (~95 w)

P-E-E-L: Point, Explain, Example, Link.

P3
Body 2 (~95 w)

P-E-E-L again. Different idea.

P4
Conclusion (~40 w)

Signal end → summarise → restate thesis.

⑤ Architect Drills

Three short drills targeting your highest-leverage skills.

Drill A · Paraphrase

Rewrite the Prompt

Pick the strongest paraphrase. Strongest = different vocabulary AND different sentence structure, while keeping the meaning.

Original prompt: "Some people believe that universities should focus on providing academic skills, while others think they should also prepare students for the workplace."
Drill B · Task 1 Overview

Which is the Band 7+ Overview?

A line graph shows the proportion of people aged 65+ in USA, Sweden, Japan from 1980 to 2020.

% of population aged 65+ 1980 2000 2020 USA 15 → 16 → 19 (steady rise) Sweden 14 → 15 → 18 (gradual rise) Japan 9 → 17 → 25 (sharp rise — overtakes both)
Drill C · Cohesion

Pick the Right Linker

Mechanical linkers ("Firstly", "Secondly", "Thirdly") are a Band 6 tell. Pick something that fits the actual logic.
