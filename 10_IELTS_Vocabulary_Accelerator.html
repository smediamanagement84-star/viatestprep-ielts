④ The Function Word Bank

Topic vocabulary tells you what to talk about. This section tells you how to talk about anything — the small set of jobs every sentence in a Task 2 essay or Speaking answer actually does: showing importance, change, cause, contrast, and opinion. Master these ten functions and you stop repeating "important", "big", "because", and "but" in every answer, on every topic, for the rest of the test.

Showing Importance

instead of "important"

Adjectives: vital · crucial · paramount · indispensable · pivotal

Phrases: plays a pivotal role in · cannot be overstated · of paramount importance

"Access to clean water is of paramount importance to public health."

Showing Increase

instead of "go up"

Verbs: rise · surge · soar · climb · escalate

Nouns/adverbs: a sharp/steady/gradual rise · a dramatic surge

"Obesity rates have soared over the past two decades, particularly among children."

Showing Decrease

instead of "go down"

Verbs: decline · plummet · dwindle · diminish · taper off

Nouns/adverbs: a steady decline · a sharp drop · dwindling resources

"Fish stocks have dwindled sharply as a result of decades of overfishing."

Showing Cause

instead of "because"

Phrases: owing to · stems from · is attributable to · a contributing factor is

Verbs: trigger · give rise to · precipitate

"The rise in remote work stems largely from advances in digital communication."

Showing Effect / Result

instead of "so"

Phrases: as a consequence · this in turn · which ultimately leads to

Verbs: result in · culminate in · exacerbate

"Chronic underinvestment in public transport has, in turn, exacerbated traffic congestion."

Showing Contrast

instead of "but"

Linkers: whereas · nevertheless · that said · on the other hand

Phrases: in spite of this · despite these benefits

"Renewable energy is becoming more affordable; that said, storage technology remains a major obstacle."

Giving Examples

instead of "for example"

Phrases: a case in point is · this is exemplified by · to illustrate

Verbs: exemplify · epitomise · illustrate

"Singapore's public housing scheme exemplifies how government policy can directly reduce homelessness."

Expressing Opinion

instead of "I think"

Phrases: it could be argued that · from my perspective · I am inclined to believe

Verbs: contend · maintain · advocate

"I am inclined to believe that the benefits of automation outweigh the risks to employment."

Describing General Change

instead of "change"

Verbs: transform · shift · evolve · overhaul · revamp

Nouns: a paradigm shift · a fundamental transformation

"Streaming services have fundamentally transformed how audiences consume media."

Showing Degree / Extent

instead of "very"

Adverbs: considerably · substantially · markedly · to a large extent

Phrases: a significant proportion of · overwhelmingly

"A substantial proportion of graduates now work in fields unrelated to their degree."
How to actually use this: don't try to memorise all ten at once. Pick one function per essay you practise, force yourself to use two of its phrases, and move to the next. By the time you've cycled through all ten a few times, they stop being a list and start being your natural way of writing.
