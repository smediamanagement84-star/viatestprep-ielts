④ The Apostrophe — 2 Jobs

The mark students misuse most often. The fix takes 5 minutes — and saves a band point in any essay where apostrophes appear.

Job 1

Possession

Show that something belongs to someone or something.

❌ Wrong: the students results
✅ Right: the student's results
❌ Wrong: the womens organisation
✅ Right: the women's organisation
Plural nouns ending in -s: add ONLY the apostrophe → "the students' results" (= results of multiple students).
Job 2

Contractions

Mark omitted letters when shortening: it is → it's, do not → don't.

⚠ Avoid in academic writing: it's, don't, won't
✅ Use full forms: it is, do not, will not
IELTS rule: Avoid contractions in Writing Task 2. They're conversational, not academic.
⚠

The Biggest Apostrophe Trap: it's vs its

it's = contraction of "it is" or "it has". It's raining = It IS raining.
its (no apostrophe) = possessive. The dog wagged its tail = the tail belonging to the dog.

Quick test: If you can replace it with "it is" or "it has", use it's. Otherwise, use its.

⑦ IELTS Use-Rules — Quick Reference

✅ Use freely in Writing Task 2
  • Full stop — ends every complete idea
  • Comma — all 4 jobs (lists, intros, FANBOYS, non-essential info)
  • Semicolon — once or twice per essay, max
  • Colon — to introduce a list or explanation
  • Question mark — only for rhetorical questions in intros
⚠ Use sparingly or avoid
  • Exclamation mark — never in academic writing
  • Contractions (don't, can't) — never
  • Parentheses — use commas or dashes instead
  • Ellipsis (…) — never in your essay
  • Hyphenated compounds — use sparingly, always be sure
