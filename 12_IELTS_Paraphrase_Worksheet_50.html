Paraphrase the Prompt — 50 Practice Questions

Paraphrasing is the first move of every Task 2 essay. A strong paraphrase changes both vocabulary AND sentence structure, while keeping the meaning identical. 50 prompts below — 10 per essay type. Write your own paraphrase, then reveal two sample versions to compare.

📘 The 3-step paraphrase method — click to expand
1. Swap key words

Replace 3–4 key nouns, verbs and adjectives with synonyms. people → individuals · think → believe · important → vital · big → significant

2. Reshape the sentence

Move clauses around. Turn "Some people think X" into "It is sometimes argued that X" or "X is a view held by some". Use a subordinator (while, although, despite the fact that).

3. Keep the meaning

If the prompt has two views, KEEP both. If it asks for an opinion, signal you'll give one. Never add new ideas or drop key information.
