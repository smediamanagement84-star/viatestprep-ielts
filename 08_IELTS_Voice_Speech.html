Bonus · Voice & Speech

Two grammar moves that separate Band 6 from Band 7+

Active vs Passive Voice. Direct vs Reported Speech. Both are tested across all four IELTS skills — most prominently in Writing Task 1 (process descriptions) and Speaking Part 2 (storytelling).

① Active vs Passive Voice

In active, the subject performs the action. In passive, the subject receives it. Use both — that's range.

Active
Subject + Verb + Object

Direct, clear, forceful. Default for Speaking and most of Writing Task 2.

The chef prepares the meal.
Passive
Object + be (tense) + past participle (+ by + agent)

Formal, academic. Use when the action matters more than the doer.

The meal is prepared by the chef.
When to switch to PASSIVE
  • Writing Task 1 process diagrams: "The materials are sorted." (Doer doesn't matter.)
  • Reporting data objectively: "An increase was observed in 2018."
  • Formal tone: "It is believed that…" instead of "I believe that…"
  • Hiding the doer when unknown or irrelevant.

② Direct vs Reported Speech

Direct = exact words in quotes. Reported = paraphrasing what was said. Reported is what you'll use most in IELTS — Speaking Part 2 stories, Writing Task 2 ("Some people argue…").

Direct

Exact words in quotation marks.

She said, "I will be there by 8 PM."
Reported

Paraphrased — tense backshifts, pronouns and times shift.

She said that she would be there by 8 PM.
3 things change when you go from Direct → Reported
1. Tense backshifts one step into the past (see table below).
2. Pronouns shift: "I" → "he/she". "We" → "they".
3. Time/place markers shift: "today" → "that day". "tomorrow" → "the next day". "here" → "there".

③ The Tense Backshift Reference

Memorise this table. It's the single most useful page for reported speech.

Direct→ReportedExample
Present Simple→Past Simple"I work" → "He said he worked"
Present Continuous→Past Continuous"I am reading" → "She said she was reading"
Past Simple→Past Perfect"We lived" → "They said they had lived"
Present Perfect→Past Perfect"I have been" → "He said he had been"
Past Continuous→Past Perfect Continuous"I was walking" → "He said he had been walking"
Past Perfect→Past Perfect (no change)"I had eaten" → "She said she had eaten"
will→would"I will be there" → "She said she would be there"
can→could"I can swim" → "He said he could swim"
may→might"I may be late" → "She said she might be late"
must→had to"You must finish" → "He said I had to finish"
should / could / might / would / ought to→(no change)"You should see a doctor" → "She said I should see a doctor"
Backshift NOT needed when: reporting verb is in present ("She SAYS…"), the statement is a universal truth, or the situation is still currently true.
