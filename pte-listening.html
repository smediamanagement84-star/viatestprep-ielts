PTE Listening Focus
1–2 items Audio 60–90 sec 10 min to write 50–70 words Scores: Listening + Writing

An audio recording of 60–90 seconds plays once. You write a 50–70 word summary in 10 minutes. Scores both Listening AND Writing simultaneously.

What The AI Scores
SUB-SKILL WHAT IT MEASURES
Content Does the summary capture the main point and key details accurately?
Form Is it within 50–70 words? Multiple sentences are allowed.
Spelling Every word correctly spelled. Choose British or American consistently.
4-Sentence SST Template
SENTENCE 1 — Topic + Main Point:
"The lecture discusses [main topic], with particular focus on [specific aspect]."

SENTENCE 2 — Central Claim:
"The speaker argues that [central claim], providing evidence that [supporting evidence]."

SENTENCE 3 — Detail:
"Furthermore, [second important point], which highlights [significance]."

SENTENCE 4 — Conclusion:
"In conclusion, [key takeaway / recommendation]."
2–3 items ⚠ NEGATIVE MARKING

Negative marking applies. Incorrect selections deduct marks. Only select options you are highly confident about.

2–3 items Scores: Listening + Writing

An audio recording plays. Type the missing words in the blanks. Focus heavily on spelling, as every typo costs writing marks.

1–2 items Scores: Listening + Reading

An audio recording plays. Select the summary option that best represents the key points of the recording.

3–4 items Scores: Listening + Writing Crucial task

A short sentence plays once. Type the sentence exactly as you heard it. Every correct word earns 1 point. This is one of the highest-scoring tasks in the entire test.

How To Attempt — Step By Step
1
Listen intently without typing
Focus all concentration on the voice. Do not look down or touch the keyboard during playback.
2
Type immediately while fresh
Write down the words in order. Check spelling, capitalization, and punctuation carefully.
3
Use spelling synonyms if unsure
If you're unsure whether a word was singular or plural, some systems allow writing both (e.g. "student students") for partial credit.
Spelling Traps
COMMON MISTAKES
▸
Double letters: necessary · accommodation · commitment · beginning
▸
Homophones: their vs there · affect vs effect · principal vs principle
