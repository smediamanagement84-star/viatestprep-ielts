Agree or Disagree Essay Template

A print-ready writing architecture designed for Band 7.5+ performance. Study the paragraph structures, memorize the anchor phrases, and review the model essay.

■ 1. The 4-Paragraph Layout Grid

Paragraph 1: Introduction

  • Sentence 1: Paraphrase the prompt context
  • Sentence 2: Clear thesis statement (Agree/Disagree)
  • Sentence 3: Outline sentence (Preview main points)

Paragraph 2: Body 1 (First Argument)

  • Sentence 1: Topic sentence stating first supporting reason
  • Sentence 2: Deeper explanation of the point
  • Sentence 3: Concrete example or statistical reference
  • Sentence 4: Concluding link (Result/Impact)

Paragraph 3: Body 2 (Second Argument)

  • Sentence 1: Topic sentence stating second supporting reason
  • Sentence 2: Deeper explanation of the point
  • Sentence 3: Concrete example or case study
  • Sentence 4: Concluding link (Result/Impact)

Paragraph 4: Conclusion

  • Sentence 1: Restate thesis in a fresh way (No copying)
  • Sentence 2: Summarize body paragraph arguments
  • Sentence 3: Final prediction or recommendation

■ 2. Sentence-by-Sentence Formulas

Context Paraphrase Hook

"It is frequently argued that [paraphrase prompt topic], while others believe that [alternative view]."

Thesis & Preview Statement

"I completely agree/disagree with this notion because [Reason A] and [Reason B]. This essay will examine both aspects of the issue."

Body Topic Sentences

"On the one hand, the primary reason supporting [Argument 1] is that..." or "Furthermore, another critical factor to consider is..."

Example Introducers

"A salient example of this is..." or "Recent empirical studies conducted by [Institution] demonstrate that..."

■ 3. Band 8+ Model Essay in Action

Essay Prompt: Some people think that universities should focus on providing graduates with the practical skills needed in the workplace. Others think that the true function of a university should be to teach knowledge for its own sake, regardless of its employment utility. To what extent do you agree or disagree?

In contemporary society, debate surrounds whether tertiary institutions should orient their curricula primarily toward vocational training, or if they should remain bastions of pure academic knowledge. I strongly agree that the primary function of higher education must be to equip students with practical skills, since this directly enhances graduate employability and drives economic productivity.

The primary argument in favor of vocational-oriented education is that it directly addresses the challenges of the modern job market. With rising tuition fees and increasingly competitive employment sectors, graduates require immediate, tangible skills to secure professional positions. A salient example of this is the technology sector, where graduates with practical coding experience are hired significantly faster than those with purely theoretical computer science backgrounds. Consequently, this highlights the necessity of aligning university curricula with the demands of employers to ensure students receive a high return on their educational investment.

Furthermore, from a macroeconomic perspective, universities that prioritize industry-specific skills serve as critical engines of economic growth. When educational frameworks produce job-ready professionals, businesses can innovate and scale without incurring heavy retraining costs. A study conducted by the Munich Institute for Economics demonstrated that countries with strong university-industry partnerships experienced a 15% increase in technological innovation index scores. Therefore, it is clear that tertiary education focused on utility rather than abstract theory yields substantial public benefits.

In conclusion, I firmly believe that universities must prioritize practical skills over theoretical knowledge for its own sake. By focusing on employment utility, universities not only safeguard the career prospects of graduates but also bolster national economic index indexes. Looking ahead, it is recommended that governments incentivize universities to co-design curricula with leading corporate sectors.
