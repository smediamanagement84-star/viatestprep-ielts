PTE Speaking Mastery
6–7 items 30–40 sec each Scores: Speaking + Reading Highest value task

A text of up to 60 words appears on screen. After a countdown and beep you read it aloud. This is the single most impactful task in PTE — it scores Speaking (fluency, pronunciation) AND Reading (accuracy) simultaneously.

What The AI Scores
SUB-SKILL WHAT IT MEASURES
Content Percentage of words read correctly and in order
Oral Fluency Natural rhythm, smooth chunked phrasing — no hesitations or false starts
Pronunciation Correct sound production, word stress, and connected speech
How To Attempt — Step By Step
1
Use all prep time wisely
Skim the full text once. Identify unfamiliar words and rehearse them silently. Mark natural pause points at commas and full stops.
2
Begin immediately after the beep
Start within 1 second. Dead air at the beginning registers as a fluency issue.
3
Read in meaning chunks — not word by word
Group words into phrases: "The government / has announced / new investment policies / for rural development." Pausing at punctuation creates natural rhythm.
4
Stress content words, reduce function words
Emphasise nouns, main verbs, adjectives. Reduce articles (a, the), prepositions (of, in). This is how native English rhythm works.
5
Never stop or self-correct
If you mispronounce a word, keep going. Self-correction destroys fluency more than the original error.
Chunking Guide
READ IN MEANING CHUNKS: ✗ Wrong: "The / risk / of / flooding / in / coastal / areas / is / increasing."
✓ Correct: The risk of flooding / in coastal areas / is increasing / at an alarming rate. Pause rule: Comma = brief pause (0.3s) · Full stop = longer pause (0.5s)
Stress rule: Give extra volume + length to the main noun in each chunk:
"The RISK of flooding / in COASTal areas / is inCREASing."
Pronunciation Rules For 79+
KEY RULES
▸
Linking: Connect words — "turn_it_on", "an_apple", "at_all". Choppy delivery drops fluency.
▸
Schwa: Unstressed vowels reduce to /ə/ — "about" = /əˈbaʊt/.
▸
Syllable stress: pho-TOG-ra-phy · e-CON-o-my · gov-ERN-ment.
What To Avoid
COMMON MISTAKES
✗
Going silent mid-text — the AI cuts the microphone after 3 seconds of silence.
✗
Repeating or correcting yourself — self-correction destroys fluency.
✗
Skipping words or lines — each skipped word reduces content score.

10–12 items 15 sec to respond Scores: Speaking + Listening 2nd highest value task

An audio sentence plays once. You must repeat it exactly — same words, same order, same rhythm. With 10–12 items it is the most frequently occurring task in the test, and simultaneously scores Speaking and Listening.

What The AI Scores
SUB-SKILL WHAT IT MEASURES
Content Percentage of original words reproduced correctly and in the right order
Oral Fluency Smooth, uninterrupted delivery at natural pace — no choppy hesitation
Pronunciation Correct sounds and stress patterns matching the original speaker
How To Attempt — Step By Step
1
Listen without writing or typing
Full attention on audio only. Writing shorthand means you will miss the second half of the sentence.
2
Use the 4-Block Memory Method
Anchor the first 2-3 words → capture subject + verb core → hold the last 3-4 words → reconstruct.
3
Speak immediately after audio ends
Begin within 1 second. The microphone activates automatically. Pausing longer than 2 seconds costs fluency.
Memory Template
Example: "The government has allocated significant funding for renewable energy development." BLOCK 1 (Anchor): The government has
BLOCK 2 (Core): allocated significant funding
BLOCK 3 (Tail): for renewable energy development
3–4 items 25 sec prep · 40 sec speak Scores: Speaking High value task

An image appears — bar chart, line graph, pie chart, table, map, process diagram, or photo. You have 25 seconds to study it then 40 seconds to describe it. You must fill the full 40 seconds without stopping. Pearson's AI measures your oral fluency, pronunciation, and content coverage.

What The AI Scores
SUB-SKILL WHAT IT MEASURES
Content Did you cover the key features, overall trend, and speak for the full time?
Oral Fluency Natural rhythm — no dead silence, smooth transitions between points
Pronunciation Clear, intelligible delivery of data points and descriptive vocabulary
How To Attempt — Step By Step
1
During prep: build a 5-sentence skeleton on notepad
Write down: ① What is it? ② Overall trend ③ Highest value ④ Lowest / contrast ⑤ Conclusion. 5 sentences × 8 seconds = 40 seconds perfectly filled.
2
Always start with introduction — never with data
Open by stating what the image shows: "The bar chart illustrates..." This signals to the AI you understood the visual structure first.
3
State the overall trend as your second sentence
The Overview is the most important sentence. Always give a general summary before diving into specific numbers.
4
Select 2–3 key data points — not all of them
Trying to mention every data point causes rushed monotone delivery. Pick highest, lowest, and one interesting comparison. Use "approximately" for unclear values.
5
Always end with a conclusion sentence
If time is running short: "In conclusion, this data highlights a clear pattern of..." Never stop mid-thought; a proper conclusion ensures high content scores.
Universal 5-Sentence Template
① INTRODUCTION (5–6 sec): "The [image type: bar chart / line graph / pie chart / table / map / diagram] illustrates [main topic] [time period / location if shown]." ② OVERVIEW — Key Trend (7–8 sec): "Overall, it is evident that [most striking finding or dominant pattern]." ③ HIGHEST / MOST PROMINENT (8 sec): "Notably, [category with highest value] recorded the highest figure at approximately [value], [followed by / compared with / while] [contrast detail]." ④ LOWEST / CONTRAST (7–8 sec): "In contrast, [lowest category] showed the smallest figure at [value], [additional observation]." ⑤ CONCLUSION (6–7 sec): "In conclusion, the [image type] clearly demonstrates that [key takeaway — link to real-world implication]."
Vocabulary Banks
INCREASES
rose sharply · surged to · climbed steadily · increased dramatically · peaked at · reached a high of
DECREASES
declined sharply · fell dramatically · dropped to · plummeted · hit a low of · decreased steadily
STABLE
remained stable · levelled off · fluctuated around · showed little change · stayed relatively constant
COMPARISON & DIALS
in contrast · compared with · whereas · while · on the other hand · significantly higher than
PROCESS DIAGRAMS
first · then · subsequently · after which · finally · the process begins with · this leads to
MAPS & SPATIAL
was demolished · was replaced by · was built · was extended · to the north/south of · in the centre
What To Avoid
MISTAKES TO AVOID
✗
Going silent — if you run out of content, repeat the overview in different words to fill time.
✗
Describing every single data point — causes rushed monotone delivery that drops oral fluency.
✗
Inventing precise numbers not visible in the image — use "approximately" or "roughly".
✗
Using past tense for process diagrams — always present simple passive: "water IS filtered".
✗
Reading numbers too fast — "forty-five percent" should take a full second; rush it and it sounds like gibberish.
2–3 items Audio up to 90 sec 10 sec prep · 40 sec speak Scores: Speaking + Listening

An audio lecture plays — sometimes with a still image on screen. After it ends you have 10 seconds to prepare then 40 seconds to summarise it in your own words. Focus on the main topic, central argument, and 2-3 supporting points.

What The AI Scores
SUB-SKILL WHAT IT MEASURES
Content Did you accurately capture the main topic, central claim, and key supporting points?
Oral Fluency Smooth connected delivery — no long pauses, hesitations, or choppy word-by-word output
Pronunciation Clear production of English sounds and natural word stress
Note-Taking System During Audio
WHAT TO WRITE ON YOUR NOTEPAD
▸
First 10 sec: Write the TOPIC — 1–2 words only. Always stated early in the lecture.
▸
Middle section: Note key nouns, numbers, names, technical terms. Hardest to recall from memory.
▸
Last 10 sec: Note the conclusion or recommendation — speakers always signal it at the end.
▸
Use symbols: → = leads to · + = also · ↑ = increase · ↓ = decrease · ? = question raised.
▸
Write KEYWORDS only — do NOT attempt full sentences. Your listening will suffer.
5-Sentence Response Template
① TOPIC (5 sec): "The lecture discusses [main topic]." ② MAIN ARGUMENT (8 sec): "The speaker argues that [central claim], explaining that [main reasoning]." ③ SUPPORTING DETAIL 1 (8 sec): "Furthermore, [first key supporting point or evidence], which [significance or implication]." ④ SUPPORTING DETAIL 2 (8 sec): "The speaker also mentions that [second key point], highlighting [why it matters]." ⑤ CONCLUSION (7 sec): "In conclusion, the lecture highlights the importance of [key takeaway]."
SAMPLE RESPONSE (~40 seconds)
The lecture discusses the psychological effects of social media on teenagers. The speaker argues that excessive screen time is directly linked to rising anxiety levels, explaining that algorithmic design deliberately extends engagement beyond healthy limits. Furthermore, studies show a 40% rise in reported mental health issues among adolescents over the past decade. The speaker also mentions that parents and schools are ill-equipped to counter this without policy intervention. In conclusion, the lecture highlights the importance of urgent regulatory frameworks to protect young users from harmful content.
What To Avoid
MISTAKES TO AVOID
✗
Starting with "I heard…" or "He said…" — use "The lecture discusses…" or "The speaker argues…".
✗
Using first person to paraphrase: "He said he thought climate was bad." — maintain academic reporting register.
✗
Trying to reproduce every detail — 4–5 key points delivered fluently beats 8 points delivered poorly.
✗
Going silent if you miss a point — say "the lecture also explores related aspects of this field" and continue.
✗
Stopping before 40 seconds — always fill the time even if you need to repeat the conclusion.
5–6 items 3–9 sec audio 10 sec to respond Scores: Listening + Speaking

A short question plays — sometimes accompanied by an image. You answer with one word or a very short phrase. Questions test general world knowledge, vocabulary, and common facts. One correct word = full marks.

How To Attempt — Step By Step
1
Focus on the question word
What is…? / Who…? / What do you call…? The question word tells you exactly what type of answer is needed.
2
Answer immediately — within 1 second
The microphone window is short. Hesitation wastes recording time. Say your answer confidently.
3
One or two words maximum
Do not explain or justify. Extra words do not help — the AI scores on the single correct answer word.
4
Always attempt — never stay silent
A wrong answer scores zero — same as silence. But confident delivery at least keeps your oral fluency metrics intact.
Common Question Categories + Sample Answers
QUESTION TYPE EXAMPLE → ANSWER
Opposites "What is the opposite of expensive?" → cheap / inexpensive
Definitions "What do you call a doctor who treats children?" → paediatrician
Geography "What is the largest ocean?" → Pacific
Science "What gas do plants absorb?" → carbon dioxide / CO₂
Tools "What measures temperature?" → thermometer
Professions "Who writes prescriptions?" → doctor / physician
Language "What is the plural form of child?" → children
Calendar "How many days are in a leap year?" → 366
Preparation tip: Study a list of 200 common PTE ASQ answers — these questions repeat from a limited pool. Recognising the question instantly builds response speed and confidence significantly.
What To Avoid
MISTAKES TO AVOID
✗
Long answers: "I think it might be a thermometer because it measures temperature..." — one word only.
✗
Saying "I don't know" or staying silent — always guess a likely word.
✗
Over-thinking — ASQ answers are almost always the first common word that comes to mind.
2–3 items (Academic) 2–4 items (Core) 10s prep · 40s speak Scores: Speaking

You hear and read a real-world situation on screen. After the audio ends, you have 10 seconds to prepare (Academic) or 20 seconds (Core), then 40 seconds to speak. Respond naturally — as you would to a real person in that situation.

What The AI Scores
SUB-SKILL WHAT IT MEASURES
Content Does your response address all elements of the situation fully and appropriately?
Oral Fluency Natural, conversational rhythm — not academic, not robotic
Pronunciation Clear, intelligible delivery at a natural conversational pace
How To Attempt — Step By Step
1
During prep: identify 3 key elements
① WHO am I talking to? (neighbour, manager, customer) ② WHAT happened? ③ WHAT is my response? (apologise, request, explain, offer solution)
2
Open by acknowledging the situation naturally
Start with a natural opener relevant to the audience: "Hi, I just wanted to come over and..." / "Good morning, I'm calling about..."
3
State your main response clearly
Make your key point in one clear sentence, then support it with a brief reason or explanation from the prompt details.
4
Offer a specific solution or next step
Strong responses always include a concrete action: "I'll make sure this doesn't happen again." / "Could we arrange a time to discuss this?"
5
Close politely and invite follow-up
End with: "Please let me know if there's anything else I can do." This signals communicative competence to the AI.
Universal 4-Part Template
OPENER — Address audience directly: "[Hi / Hello / Good morning / Excuse me], I [wanted to reach out / am calling / came over] about [the situation from the prompt]." MAIN RESPONSE — Your key action: "I [sincerely apologise for / wanted to let you know that / would like to request / need to explain] [specific detail] because [brief reason]." SOLUTION / NEXT STEP: "I think the best way to [resolve / address / move forward] would be to [specific practical step], and I [will ensure / can arrange / would appreciate] [outcome]." POLITE CLOSE: "Please [let me know / feel free to contact me / don't hesitate to reach out] if you need anything further."
Situation Type Quick-Reference
SITUATION TYPE KEY LANGUAGE
Apologising I sincerely apologise for… · I take full responsibility… · It won't happen again.
Making a request I was wondering if you could… · Would it be possible to… · I'd really appreciate it if…
Giving information I wanted to let you know that… · Just to update you… · Please be aware that…
Solving a problem The best approach would be to… · I suggest we… · One way to resolve this is…
Accepting / Declining I'd be happy to… · Unfortunately, I'm unable to… · I'm afraid I won't be able to…
What To Avoid
MISTAKES TO AVOID
✗
Using academic or formal essay language — this is real-world conversation, use natural speech register.
✗
Ignoring details from the prompt — every detail in the situation description must be addressed.
✗
Stopping before 40 seconds — add a polite follow-up or reiterate your solution to fill the remaining time.
✗
Memorising one fixed response — situations vary, use the template structure not the exact words.
2–3 items Audio up to 3 min 2 min to respond Scores: Listening + Speaking

You listen to a 3-speaker conversation (up to 3 minutes) then have 2 minutes to summarise the main viewpoints. This is a double-skill task that scores both Listening and Speaking simultaneously.

What The AI Scores
SUB-SKILL WHAT IT MEASURES
Content Did you accurately capture the key viewpoints all three speakers contributed?
Oral Fluency Smooth, uninterrupted 2-minute delivery at a natural pace
Pronunciation Clear production maintained throughout the full response
Note-Taking System During Audio
WHAT TO WRITE ON YOUR NOTEPAD
▸
First 15 sec: Write the TOPIC — 1–2 words. Always established at the very start.
▸
Speaker A: Note their main position or key argument — 3–5 keywords.
▸
Speaker B: Do they agree, counter, or add a new angle? Note their key point.
▸
Speaker C: Note their contribution — especially if they propose a conclusion or solution.
▸
You do NOT need to track who said what — just capture 3 distinct viewpoints or ideas.
5-Sentence Response Template
① INTRODUCE TOPIC (8 sec): "The group discussed [main topic], exploring a range of perspectives on [specific aspect]." ② FIRST VIEWPOINT (18 sec): "One speaker argued that [Viewpoint A], emphasising that [key reason or supporting detail]." ③ SECOND VIEWPOINT (18 sec): "Another participant [agreed / disagreed / extended this] by pointing out that [Viewpoint B], which [adds to / contrasts with the first view]." ④ THIRD VIEWPOINT (18 sec): "A third perspective raised was that [Viewpoint C], suggesting that [implication or proposed solution]." ⑤ OVERALL CONCLUSION (10 sec): "Overall, the discussion highlighted [central tension or shared conclusion] as a key issue requiring [further attention / a balanced approach]."
SAMPLE RESPONSE — Topic: Remote Work
The group discussed the growing trend of remote work, exploring its impact on productivity and workplace culture. One speaker argued that working from home dramatically improves work-life balance and reduces commuting stress, citing measurable output gains. Another participant disagreed, pointing out that remote work weakens team cohesion and makes onboarding new staff extremely difficult. A third perspective raised was that a hybrid model offers the most practical solution, preserving flexibility while maintaining social bonds. Overall, the discussion highlighted that while remote work brings genuine individual benefits, a balanced organisational policy is needed to address its wider professional drawbacks.
What To Avoid
MISTAKES TO AVOID
✗
Attributing specific quotes to specific speakers — summarise the ideas, not who said them.
✗
Only covering two of the three viewpoints — all three must feature for full Content marks.
✗
Adding your own personal opinion — this is a neutral summary of what the speakers discussed.
✗
Running out of time — 2 minutes = approximately 5 sentences at a natural pace. Plan your structure.
✗
Going silent if you miss a point — say "the discussion also explored related dimensions of this topic" as a bridge.
Training tip: Watch BBC HARDtalk, Al Jazeera panel discussions, or TEDx debates. After each one, summarise in 2 minutes out loud. This builds the exact listening-to-speaking conversion skill tested here.
