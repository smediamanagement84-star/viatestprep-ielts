A Band 7+ requires strict structure, logical paragraphing, and precise lexical control.

② The 4 Pillars of Band 7+ Writing

Your essay is evaluated on four distinct criteria. To score Band 7 or above, you must hit all four marks.

🎯 Task Response (25%) - Fully address all parts of the prompt. - Present a clear, consistent position throughout the essay. - Support your main ideas with relevant explanations and examples. Avoid generalisations.
🔗 Coherence & Cohesion (25%) - Organise your ideas logically across exactly 4 paragraphs. - Use a range of cohesive devices (transitions, pronouns) naturally. - Ensure each paragraph has a single, clear central topic.
📚 Lexical Resource (25%) - Use less common vocabulary and precise collocations. - Paraphrase key terms from the prompt accurately. - Maintain academic tone and register (no slang, no informal contractions like *don't* or *can't*).
📐 Grammatical Range & Accuracy (25%) - Use a mix of simple, compound, and complex sentences. - Aim for error-free sentences (at least 50% should be completely error-free). - Maintain accurate tense control and correct punctuation.

③ Academic vs General Training Essay — The Facts

Many students and teachers confuse these two. Let's look at what is identical and what actually differs.

✅ 100% Identical Elements - **Timing & Length:** Both require a minimum of 250 words in 40 minutes. - **Essay Formats:** Both use the same 5 question types (Opinion, Discussion, Advantage/Disadvantage, Problem/Solution, Two-Part). - **Marking Criteria:** Both are graded on the exact same 4 descriptors (Task Response, Coherence/Cohesion, Lexical Resource, Grammar). - **Standard vs UKVI:** The actual test paper for IELTS for UKVI is identical to standard IELTS. The differences are purely administrative security rules at the test center.
🔍 The Core Difference: Topic Nature - **Academic Essays:** Deal with more abstract, academic, or global debates (e.g., genetic research, space travel, national economic policies, history preservation, artificial intelligence). - **General Training Essays:** Focus on everyday social issues, work, family, education, or personal hobbies (e.g., shopping habits, recycling in neighborhoods, child-rearing methods, television viewing, remote working). - **Vocabulary Depth:** Academic topics require a slightly higher register of specialized terminology to score Band 7+.
💡 Teacher Guidance — How to Prepare Your Students - **Teach the Same Structure:** The 4-Paragraph Blueprint and transition bank apply equally to both. - **Vary the Prompts:** Ensure Academic students practice on abstract themes (technology, urbanization) and GT students practice on local themes (community, family life). - **No Informal Tone in GT:** Warn GT students that even though the topic is an everyday subject, the essay must be written in a formal academic tone (no contractions, no casual slang).

💬 Opinion Essay (Agree/Disagree) This essay asks you to state your position clearly and defend it. You can either completely agree, completely disagree, or take a balanced view. Prompt Signal: "To what extent do you agree or disagree?" or "Do you agree or disagree?"
INTRO
Paraphrase the prompt + **Thesis Statement** (state clearly whether you agree or disagree and outline your two reasons).
BODY 1
Focus on your first main point supporting your stance (Topic sentence → Explanation → Example).
BODY 2
Focus on your second main point supporting your stance (Topic sentence → Explanation → Example).
CONCLUSION
Restate your thesis statement in different words + Summary of body points + Final closing recommendation or thought.
⚖ Discussion Essay (Discuss Both Views) You must discuss **both** opinions presented in the prompt and give your own personal opinion. Each view must get a dedicated body paragraph. Prompt Signal: "Discuss both views and give your opinion."
INTRO
Paraphrase both sides + **Thesis Statement** (state your own view on the topic).
BODY 1
Discuss View A. Explain why people hold this opinion, even if you disagree with it.
BODY 2
Discuss View B. Explain why people support this view and align this explanation with your own opinion.
CONCLUSION
Restate your opinion clearly + Summary of the core reasons for each side.
🔄 Advantages vs Disadvantages You must discuss the positive aspects and the negative aspects of a development, and state whether one side outweighs the other. Prompt Signal: "Do the advantages outweigh the disadvantages?" or "What are the advantages and disadvantages?"
INTRO
Paraphrase the prompt + **Thesis Statement** (clearly state whether the advantages outweigh the disadvantages or vice versa).
BODY 1
Discuss the advantages/benefits (Topic sentence → Detail → Example).
BODY 2
Discuss the disadvantages/drawbacks (Topic sentence → Detail → Example).
CONCLUSION
Summarize the main benefits and drawbacks + Final verdict (confirming which side outweighs).
🛠 Problem & Solution Essay Identify the main causes of a particular global issue and propose realistic, detailed solutions to address it. Prompt Signal: "What are the causes of this and what solutions can be proposed?"
INTRO
Paraphrase the prompt + **Thesis Statement** (outline the primary cause and state that solutions exist).
BODY 1
Focus on the causes/origins of the problem (usually 2 distinct causes).
BODY 2
Focus on the solutions (each solution should directly address a cause discussed in Body 1).
CONCLUSION
Summarize the core causes and solutions + A warning/hopeful final sentence about the future.
❓ Two-Part (Direct Question) Essay The prompt asks two distinct, direct questions. You must address each question in its own body paragraph. Prompt Signal: Two distinct question marks in the prompt.
INTRO
Paraphrase the prompt + **Thesis Statement** (briefly answer both questions in a single, complex sentence).
BODY 1
Answer Question 1 in full detail (Topic sentence → Explanation → Example).
BODY 2
Answer Question 2 in full detail (Topic sentence → Explanation → Example).
CONCLUSION
Restate the answers to both questions in different words + Summarising sentence.

⑤ The 4-Paragraph Essay Blueprint

Regardless of the essay type, a structured 4-paragraph layout ensures clarity and scores maximum points in Coherence & Cohesion.

INTRODUCTION
(~40-50 words)
1. Paraphrase Prompt: Rephrase the question using synonyms and varied sentence structures.
2. Thesis Statement: Present your clear point of view and state the two key sub-points you will discuss.
BODY PARAGRAPH 1
(~90-100 words)
1. Topic Sentence: Introduce the first supporting point clearly.
2. Explanation: Develop the point by explaining *how* and *why* it is true.
3. Example: Provide a specific, realistic example (e.g., studies, government statistics, or historic events) to support your explanation.
BODY PARAGRAPH 2
(~90-100 words)
1. Topic Sentence: Introduce the second supporting point clearly.
2. Explanation: Expand and justify this point in depth.
3. Example: Provide a relevant, illustrative example to ground the point.
CONCLUSION
(~30-40 words)
1. Restatement of Thesis: Rephrase your main opinion using fresh vocabulary.
2. Summary of main points: Briefly recap your two main body points.
3. Final Thought: Offer a prediction, recommendation, or warning. Do not introduce new arguments here.

⑥ The Essay Phrase Bank — Academic & GT

A curated list of formal phrases and transitional structures to build cohesion. Avoid informal phrases like *in a nutshell* or *by the way*.

🏛 Introducing the Topic

It is widely argued that…
An increasing number of experts suggest that…
A key debate in contemporary society concerns…
The issue of [topic] has become a controversial subject…

💡 Stating Your Stance

I strongly agree with this assertion because…
I hold the view that the drawbacks outweigh the benefits…
This essay argues that while some disadvantages exist, the advantages are far more significant…
I contend that this development is highly beneficial…

🔗 Presenting Points

Primary among these factors is that…
Another compelling argument in favour of this is…
Turning to the negative consequences, it is clear that…
Furthermore, it is important to consider that…

🔍 Providing Examples

A clear illustration of this is…
For instance, a recent study conducted by [Entity] revealed that…
This is exemplified by the policy implemented in [Country], which…
To illustrate this, statistics from the UK government show that…

⚖ Presenting Concessions

Admittedly, there are valid arguments on the opposing side…
Despite these obvious benefits, a major drawback remains…
Nevertheless, it can be argued that…
Although this view has some merit, it is overshadowed by…

🏁 Drawing Conclusions

In conclusion, while there are minor disadvantages, it is apparent that…
To summarise, I believe that the main cause is…
Consequently, it is recommended that governments take steps to…
Ultimately, if these measures are not implemented, it is likely that…
⚡ Academic Vocabulary Rule Never use contractions (*don't*, *won't*, *it's*) in Writing Task 2. Write them out fully (*do not*, *will not*, *it is*). Also, avoid using generic words like *good*, *bad*, or *nice*. Opt for *beneficial*, *detrimental*, or *satisfactory*.

📐 Band Descriptor Self-Check

After writing, rate your essay objectively against the examiner requirements.

Task Response

Did you write at least 250 words? Did you state a clear opinion on whether the advantages outweigh the disadvantages for the *countries they leave*?

Coherence & Cohesion

Is your essay structured into exactly 4 paragraphs (Intro, Body 1, Body 2, Conclusion)? Did you use clear transitions like *firstly*, *on the other hand*, *consequently*, or *in conclusion*?

⑨ Your 7-Day Action Plan

Writing Task 2 is won through deliberate practice of planning and drafting, not just writing blindly.

DAY 1

Prompt Analysis: Read 10 random prompts and identify their Essay Type and required parameters.

DAY 2

Idea Generation: Brainstorm and draft 5 Intro paragraphs. Must include a clear Thesis Statement.

DAY 3

Body Paragraph Practice: Write 3 Body paragraphs focusing on the Topic Sentence → Explain → Example structure.

DAY 4

Cohesion Drill: Rewrite old paragraphs using advanced transitions and pronoun referencing.

DAY 5

Conclusion Practice: Practice writing brief, impactful conclusions without adding new details.

DAY 6

Full Mock Test: Write a complete essay under timed conditions (40 minutes, 250+ words).

DAY 7

Proofreading & Review: Review your essay for grammatical slip-ups, contractions, and word choices.
