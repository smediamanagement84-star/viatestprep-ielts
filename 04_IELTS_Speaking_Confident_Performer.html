Pillar 1

Fluency & Coherence

The flow. Smooth speech without unnatural hesitation. A few thinking pauses are fine ("Well, that's interesting…"); long silent gaps are not.

Watch for "Um, um, um." Use thinking phrases instead.
Pillar 2

Lexical Resource

Precision, not pomp. Replace "things" with "equipment / belongings / possessions". Use natural collocations: heavy traffic, highly effective, deeply concerned.

Watch for Repeating "good", "bad", "interesting". Range = score.
Pillar 3

Grammatical Range & Accuracy

Mix complexity. Conditionals ("If I had…"), relative clauses ("which is something I…"), perfect tenses, passives.

Watch for Speaking only in simple present. Showcase tenses naturally.
Pillar 4

Pronunciation

NOT about your accent. About being clear. Word stress (imPORtant ≠ IMportant), sentence stress, intonation (rising/falling).

Watch for Robotic flat delivery. Add melody — pitch up at choices, down at conclusions.

③ Step 2A: Act 1 · Part 1 — The Warm-Up (Answer+1)

Friendly · direct · conversational

12 short questions about familiar topics. Goal: first impressions. Trap: 1-word answers OR 30-second monologues.

🎯 The Answer-Plus-One Rule

Give a direct answer. Then add ONE extra sentence — a reason, an example, or a detail. That's it.

❌ Band 6 · "Yes/no"
Q: "Do you enjoy cooking?"
A: "Yes, I do."
✅ Band 7+ · Answer+1
A: "Yes, I absolutely love it. I find it's a really creative way to unwind after a long day at work." (answer + reason + collocation 'unwind')

④ Step 3A: Act 2 · Part 2 — The Long Turn (P.P.F. Plan)

2-min monologue · 1-min prep

Your solo performance. The 1-minute preparation is sacred. Don't write sentences — use the P.P.F. method.

The P.P.F. Method

Past · Present · Future = built-in tense range

Cover the three time frames during your 2-minute talk. You'll naturally showcase past, present, and future tenses → instant Grammar Range win.

P · PAST
What happened? Where? When? Specific actions and feelings. Tenses: past simple, past continuous, past perfect.
P · PRESENT
Why does it matter NOW? What's the lasting effect? Tenses: present simple, present perfect.
F · FUTURE
What might happen next? Plans, hopes, predictions. Tenses: future simple, "going to", conditionals ("if I had the chance").

⑤ Step 4A: Act 3 · Part 3 — The Deep Dive (A.R.E. Answers)

Abstract · societal · analytical

Discuss abstract issues related to your Part 2 topic. The trap: giving only personal opinions. The move: A.R.E. — Answer, Reason, Example. Move from "I" to "people in general".

A · ANSWER
Direct answer to the question. State it clearly.
R · REASON
Why? Use: "This is mainly because…", "The main reason for this is…"
E · EXAMPLE
Move from "I" → "Many people". "For example, in many countries…"

📚 Hedging & Speculation Phrases

Part 3 wants speculation, not certainty. Sprinkle these naturally.

Cautious opinion
"It seems to me that…"
"I'd argue that…"
"From my perspective…"
"To my mind…"
Speculation
"It's likely that…"
"It might well be the case that…"
"There's a strong chance…"
"Conceivably, this could…"
Generalising
"Generally speaking…"
"By and large…"
"For the most part…"
"In many countries…"

🛟 Thinking Phrases — When Your Mind Goes Blank

Silence kills Fluency. Replace "um, um" with these. Use one. Buy 2 seconds of thinking time. Carry on.

Buying time:
"That's a really interesting question."
"Let me think about that for a moment."
"Hmm, I haven't thought about it that way before, but…"
Reframing:
"To put it another way…"
"What I mean to say is…"
"Or rather, …"
Self-correcting:
"Sorry, I meant…"
"Actually, let me rephrase that."
"I'd say more accurately that…"
