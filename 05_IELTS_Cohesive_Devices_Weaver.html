Bonus Lesson · Cohesion

Beautiful sentences fail when the glue is missing.

Cohesion is 12.5% of your total Writing band. It's the glue that turns a list of ideas into an argument. The biggest Band 6 mistake? Mechanical "Firstly… Secondly… Thirdly…" linkers. Today you'll learn the linkers AND the advanced moves above them.

The Two Levels
Level 1 · Linking words (Furthermore, However…). 95% of students stop here.
Level 2 · Pronoun referencing · "this + summary noun" · synonym chains. This is where 7.5+ lives.

② The 6 Linker Toolkits — by Function

Don't memorise lists. Learn one or two from each function. Tap each toolkit.

③ Three Advanced Moves (Band 7.5+)

These are what separate Band 7 from Band 7.5. The examiner won't see "Furthermore" — they'll see invisible glue.

Move 1

Pronoun Referencing

Use it, they, this, these to refer back. Avoid repeating the noun.

❌ Electric cars are popular. Electric cars reduce pollution.
✅ Electric cars are popular. They reduce pollution.
Move 2

"This + Summary Noun"

Never leave "this" lonely. Add a noun that summarises the previous idea.

❌ Temperatures are rising. This is concerning.
✅ Temperatures are rising. This trend is concerning for scientists.
Move 3

Synonym Chains

Replace repeated keywords with synonyms — links thematically AND boosts Lexical Resource.

❌ The problem is pollution. This problem is caused by traffic.
✅ The problem is pollution. This environmental issue is largely caused by heavy traffic.
