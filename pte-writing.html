PTE Writing Mastery
1–2 items 10 min each ONE sentence: 5–75 words Scores: Writing + Reading

A passage of up to 300 words appears. You must write a single summary sentence of 5–75 words in 10 minutes. Scores both Writing and Reading simultaneously — one of the highest-value tasks in the test.

CRITICAL WARNING
Form is the highest-weighted criterion
✗
If you write more than one sentence (even separated by a semicolon), or fewer than 5 words, you score ZERO on Form. One complete sentence only.
How To Attempt — Step By Step
1
Read quickly to find the main idea (2 min)
The main idea is almost always in the first and last paragraphs. Skim for the author's central claim — not a supporting example.
2
Draft your sentence using a complex structure (5 min)
Use a subordinate clause to connect the main argument to a supporting detail. Aim for 40–60 words.
3
Proofread and word count (3 min)
Count words. Verify there is no full stop creating a second sentence. Replace copied phrases with synonyms.
Sentence Structure Blueprints
STRUCTURE 1 — Although / While contrast: "Although [concession / opposing point], the passage argues that [main claim], emphasising that [supporting detail]." STRUCTURE 2 — The passage discusses: "The passage discusses [topic], highlighting the fact that [main argument] and suggesting that [implication]."
1–2 items 20 min each 200–300 words Scores: All 6 Writing sub-skills

A topic is given. You write an argumentative essay of 200–300 words in 20 minutes. This task tests the widest range of writing sub-skills in the entire test.

Universal 4-Paragraph Template
INTRO (~50 words): "In recent years, [topic/issue] has become a matter of considerable debate. While some argue that [opposing view], I firmly believe that [your position] for two key reasons." BODY 1 — Main reason (~80 words): "The principal reason for this view is that [reason 1]. This is because [explanation]. For instance, [specific example]. This demonstrates that [link to thesis]." BODY 2 — Counter + rebuttal (~80 words): "Admittedly, some argue that [counter-argument]. However, this view overlooks the fact that [rebuttal]. While it is true that [concession], the evidence suggests that [reinforce position]." CONCLUSION (~40 words): "In conclusion, it is evident that [restate position]. [Governments/Individuals] should [recommended action]. Only through [solution] can [outcome] be achieved."
Grammar Structures To Include For 79+
KEY MOVES
▸
Passive voice: "It has been demonstrated that..."
▸
Conditionals: "If governments fail to act, consequences will follow."
▸
Inversion: "Not only does X reduce costs, but it also increases output."

2–3 items 9 min each 80–120 words PTE Core only

A prompt describes a situation requiring an email. You write a formal or semi-formal email of 80–120 words in 9 minutes. Scored on email conventions (subject line, salutation, body, sign-off).

Formal Email Template
Subject: [Clear, concise description]

Dear [Mr/Ms Last Name],

I am writing to [enquire about / request] [subject].

[Address Point 1]. Furthermore, [address Point 2]. I would also like to mention that [address Point 3].

I would be grateful if you could [action needed]. Please do not hesitate to contact me should you require any further information.

Yours sincerely,
[Your Full Name]
